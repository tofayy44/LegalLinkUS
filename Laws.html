Know Your Rights

For Undocumented Immigrants (Illegals):

  • You have the right to remain silent. You are not obligated to speak to ICE agents or police about your immigration status, where you were born, or how you entered the U.S.
  • Do not open the door. If ICE comes to your home, ask for a signed warrant through a window or mail slot. A valid warrant must be signed by a judge — NOT just ICE paperwork.
  • Carry a Know Your Rights card. You can carry a card that states your right to remain silent and request a lawyer. This helps you avoid speaking under pressure.
  • Stay calm during a stop. If you are pulled over or approached in public, stay still, don’t run, and calmly ask if you’re free to leave. If not, ask for legal representation.
  • Do not lie or provide false documents. It can make your situation worse and lead to criminal charges. Stay silent and demand a lawyer.
  • You have the right to an attorney. While not guaranteed at government expense in immigration court, you can hire one or request resources for free legal clinics.
  • Do not sign anything you don’t understand. ICE may try to get you to sign a "voluntary departure" — this can result in being banned from reentry. Always ask for a lawyer first.

For Trappers (Hustlers):

  • 4th Amendment Protection: Law enforcement cannot legally search you, your vehicle, or your home without a warrant, consent, or probable cause.
  • Don’t talk business on the phone. Always assume you're being listened to. Anything you say can be used in a case against you later.
  • Ask for a lawyer immediately upon arrest. Don’t answer any questions, even if you think it’s harmless.
  • Don’t consent to any search. Verbally say, “I do not consent to a search.” It might help your case later if evidence was taken illegally.

For Highway Racers:

  • Keep your registration and license clean. Many racing charges get stacked with suspended license or expired tags — avoid easy losses.
  • If stopped, stay respectful. Record the interaction if you can. Don’t argue or flee. Ask if you're being detained and state your rights clearly.
  • Vehicle impound? Ask for a tow release hearing immediately. You may be able to recover your vehicle before large fees hit.

Important Reminders:

  • Remain silent. This is your power. Use it.
  • Ask for a lawyer immediately.
  • Document everything you can safely.
  • Never sign anything you don’t understand.
